House Blend Coffee

Coffee is consumed all over the world, from growing to production and distribution. There are so many blends, brands and beans, that it can be hard to understand how each cup of coffee is different from the other. A coffee blend is the result of a combination of two different beans to produce different flavors and strengths. Mixtures are not standard in the coffee industry, making it difficult to fully understand the basic differences between cups.

French Roast

French Roast is the name given to the degree of roasting of the coffee beans, resulting in the formation of dark brown coffee beans. Roasted beans have a dark brown color and a glossy surface from the oil. Coffee brewed from French Roast pure beans will have a characteristic natural aroma of mute beans, especially any acidic notes. The taste is dominated by bitter-sweet shades of roast. French roasted coffee dominates coffee identified with the American West. This roasting profile is also common in American espresso.
